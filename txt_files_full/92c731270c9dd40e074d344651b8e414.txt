Station 1: 
Chicken tikka masala by Jamie Oliver 
 
Without question, chicken tikka masala is a brilliant curry that makes people very happy. Of 
course it’s inspired by fantastic Indian cooking, but is in fact an Anglo-Indian evolution, created 
to suit British palates. It has been chosen to be the most popular dish in Britain – check out the 
recipe at the bottom of the page and get grilling! 
Put the cloves, cumin and 1 heaped teaspoon each of paprika and garam masala into a small 
pan and toast for 1 minute to bring them back to life, then tip into a large bowl. Finely grate in 
the skin of 1 lemon, squeeze in all its juice, crush in the garlic, peel and finely grate in the 
ginger, and add the yoghurt and 1 teaspoon of sea salt. Cut the chicken breasts into 5cm 
chunks, then massage all that flavour into the meat. Skewer up the chicken chunks, mix them 
with lemon and chilli, but don’t squash them together too much. Place on a tray, cover with 
clingfilm and marinate in the fridge for at least 2 hours, but preferably overnight. 
 
For the sauce, peel the onions and garlic, then finely slice with the red chillies and coriander 
stalks (reserving the leaves for later). Put it all into a large casserole pan on a medium-high 
heat with a spoon of oil and cook for around 20 minutes, or until golden, stirring regularly. Add 
the ground coriander, turmeric and remaining 1 heaped teaspoon each of paprika and garam 
masala. Cook for 2 minutes, then add and toast the almonds. Pour in the tomatoes, crumble in 
the stock cube and add 300ml of boiling water. Let it cook for 5 minutes, then stir in the 
coconut milk. Let it cook for a final 40 minutes, stirring occasionally, then season to perfection. 
 
When you’re ready to cook the chicken put some oil in a screaming hot griddle pan or under a 
hot grill, turning until it’s very golden on all sides. Slice the chicken off the skewers straight into 
the sauce, reserving the lemons. Cook for 2 minutes while you use tongs to squeeze some 
lemons over the curry, to taste. Swirl through some more yoghurt, sprinkle with the coriander 
leaves, and serve with parathas or fluffy basmati rice.

Station 2: BAPS Shri Swaminarayan 

Mandir 

Welcome to BAPS Shri Swaminarayan Mandir which is 
also  called  the  Neasden  Temple.  The  total  area  of  the 
BAPS Shri Swaminarayan Mandir is 2.35 acres (almost 
1  km²).  The  temple  was  built  between  1992  and  1995 
by worshippers who did not want to receive money for 
their work. This is why it may be called a work of love. 
It  does  not  only  contain  a  prayer  hall  but  also  a  sports 
hall,  a  kitchen,  stores  and  a  library.  Attached  to  it  are 
many gardens which symbolize the fusion of London’s 
culture and the culture of the Hindus.

Bend it like Beckham 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
The film revolves around Jesminder Kaur Bhamra. She is an 18-year-old girl 
living in London.  She calls herself Jess and enjoys playing football, even 
though her parents who are Punjabi Sikhs have other plans for her: They want 
her to learn to cook Indian food and to marry an Indian man just like her older 
sister. When they find out that Jess is playing in a local team they become 
upset and forbid her to go. 
If you were Jess, what arguments would you present to your parents to 
convince them to let you play football?

Zum Abschluss der Unterrichtseinheit 
erfolgt eine Sicherung anhand eines 
Clusters an der Tafel.  Zu Beginn wird von 
der Lehrkraft gefragt, welche Teile einer 
Kultur erarbeitet wurden („Which parts 
of  culture have we dealt with?“).  Darauf 
aufbauend wird mithilfe der Schüler ein 
Cluster erstellt. Ziel ist es das Cluster 
nach den Rubriken Kulinarisches, Film, 
Musik und Architektur zu ordnen. 
Nachdem weitere Stichpunkte zu den 
Teilaspekten des Stationenlernens 
genannt wurden, erfolgt die Frage 
„Where do find culture as well?“ bzw. 
„Where is culture visible?“. Erhofft 
werden hierbei Bereiche wie 
beispielsweise Kunst, Sprache oder 
Literatur. Somit können die Lehrkräfte die 
Stunde mit einer Antwort auf die Frage 
„Was macht Kultur aus?“ abschließen 
und noch einmal einen Bogen zum 
Thema Coloured Empire Citizens 
schlagen.
