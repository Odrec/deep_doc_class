for a more realistic estimate of what regions in the picture have been focussed. Describe shortly in your

% b)
% calculate size of FWHM parameter, nop = number of pixels
radius = tand(1) * 60;
diam = radius * 2;
nop = diam / 0.025;

% c)
% calculate the kernel

kernel = make_gaussian(120, 120, nop, nop, 60, 60);

% d)
% Convolve each fixation map against the kernel

con_swan = conv2(fixmap2',kernel, 'same');
con_squirrel = conv2(fixmap1',kernel, 'same');

Comment  on  your figures: Is there a difference  between  the  fixation density maps? What would be a
possible interpretation (assuming that the results for the other stimuli are similar)?

% There is difference in the strength/number of the fixations at different
% pixels. The shown stimulus was ambiguous so participants either perceived
% the squirrel or the swan. Dependend on the percept, the fixations are
% highest at the face of either the swan or the squirrel. So we can state
% that participants look primarily at the face of the percept. The second
% most fixated pixels then belong to the other prospective face. This
% observation fits very well with the fact that we are tuned to see/detect
% faces.
